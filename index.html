About GoCorsicana

GoCorsicana is a proprietary role based platform for internal and business to business use.

Reasoning

Sales reps and managers are expected increase market share while maintaining current customer relations. However, Corsicana Mattresses (CM) processes have become outdated and non efficient, which is causing the use of unnecessary department resources. By redesigning the way Corsicana Mattress does business, GoCorsicana will be able to help customer more efficiently, increase market share, build a stronger brand, increase credibility and reputation, and become more cost efficient.

Objective

To designed and helped develop a role based online portal that aggregates reports and business applications in to one easily accessible location.

Approach

To create the portal my lead architect and business analyst first outlined the infrastructure between the company’s enterprise software, tracking software and the future web application. Once the we applications architecture was decided, I spent time researching other SAAS applications and how they outlined their services interfaces. After analyzing other applications, I was able to create low fidelity mock-ups of the portals interface using photoshop and XD. My team members and I then discussed the pros and cons of the mock-ups and decided on the most efficient interface design. Once this decision was made, I worked with my senior front end developer to build the portal’s APIs and interface.

Role

  • User Interface Designer
  • Content Designer
  • Front End Developer

Research

Research

Before creating detailed mock-ups, I reviewed other SAAS applications. My goal was to understand what type of interface design was most commonly used. By comparing other websites I was able to get insight into what other company's viewed as the best user experience.

I found out that data and text heavy SASS applications have the following commonalities.
  • Compatible with multiple browsers
  • Responsive and mobile friendly
  • Light template themes
  • Collapsible or thin navigation
  • Initial high level dashboard

Implementation

Based on my research, I was able to outline important features necessary to create a efficient portal interface.

Accessibility

  • Easy authentication and password manager
  • Intuitive navigation
  • Distraction free
  • Minimal actions
  • Compatibility with different devices and browsers

Content

  • Initial dashboard with KPIS and links to applications
  • Analytic reports
  • Business services

Designs

Transformation

The first mock-ups for both the log in screen and the portal template did not end up being the final product. With multiple versions and iterations the product owner decided on a design that incorporated both thw initial and new ideas. As the portal itself developed so did the message, and for this reason the decision was made to take advantage of the login page's real estate by including the portal's purpose and current services.

PRODUCT

Responsiveness

Making sure that the portal and its applications were compatible with different web browsers, operating systems, and devices was the biggest design requirement. This was also one of the biggest frustrations. When you design and develop interfaces you quickly realize how complicated it is to build for every device out there.

As the portal continues to grow, the more responsive and compatible it becomes. QA is not only important for application behavoir purposes, but also to make sure an element or style is supported by a browser or operating system.

FUTURE

New Look

As we began to plan for future users and applications, it was decided to re-brand the portal. The reason behind the changes were that current log in screen included content that did not pertain to future users and it was too busy. The portal template also needed to be redesigned to reduce the navigation size to allow more room for content.

About Order Tracker

Order Tracker is an application in the GoCorsicana Portal that allows the sales team and customers to easily track orders.

Reasoning

Corsicana's sales team and customers did not have a fast and efficient way to track orders. Sales reps and customers would have rely customer service for updates on current orders. Corsicana Mattress wanted to create an online application that would allow people to easily track their orders and reduce the amount of time customer service spent on relaying order updates.

Objective

To create a mobile friendly role-based application that gives the sales team and customers easy access to their order’s details and tracking information.

Approach

To create the application my team’s business analyst and lead architect outline the approach needed to pull order detail information from Corsicana Mattresses’ EP system and tracking information from the TMS system. Once the approach had been decided, our business analyst gathered requirements by discussing with the director of sales about the information needed to be accessible in the application. With the processes and requirements documented, I designed a few low fidelity mock-ups using photoshop and XD of the potential application. My team members and I then discussed and decided on the most efficient and mobile responsive interface. With the designs in hand, I built out the front-end APIs and interface for the tracking application.

After the front end and server side of the application was connected, my business analysts and I QA tested to make sure the application behaved correctly. With a few minor adjustments my team and I had built the first service in the GoCorsicana portal - the order tracking application.

Role

  • User Interface Designer
  • Content Designer
  • Front-end Developer

DESIGNS

Text Heavy

The order tracker is a data driven application, which means it is text heavy. Designing an interface for a text heavy application comes with its own challenges. One of the biggest challenges faced was how to create an aesthetically pleasing interface that was also affective and efficient when used on both desktop and mobile devices.

PRODUCT

FUTURE

Future Users

The current application has only been used by internal employees (e.g. sales reps and managers). As we approach releasing the GoCorsicana portal to external users (e.g. customers), the order tracker application must be redesigned to be faster and more user friendly. While the queries are being rewritten to be more efficient, the front end is being redesigned for two different user types. One design will be implemented for sales reps and managers, and another design will be implemented for customers.

About Customer Catalog

Customer Catalog is an application in the GoCorsicana Portal that that allows sales reps to maintain their customer’s catalogs.

Reasoning

The sales team used three different software services to maintain their customer’s business. To consolidate the number of programs the sales team was using, Corsicana Mattress decided to integrate customer catalogs into the GoCorsicana portal.

Objective

To create a mobile responsive application that allows sales reps to maintain their customer’s catalogs.

Approach

To create the application my team’s business analyst and lead architect outlined the infrastructure and requirements of the application. One the processes and requirements were documented, I designed a low fidelity mock-up using photoshop and XD of the potential application. My team members and I discussed and decided on the best user interface. The final mock-ups were given to an outsourced team who handled the development.

Role

  • User Interface Designer
  • Content Designer

DESIGNS

Mobile First

A mobile first design approach allows an application to be mobile friendly from the start. A mobile responsive application may be compatible with small devices but not optimized. Since sales reps and managers are always on the go, the applications that are pinnacle for their customer's success must be optimized for their phones not just responsive.

PRODUCT

Custom Table

The custom table my senior develop and I implemented allows all tables on GoCorsicana to be mobile friendly. As the screen's width shrinks columns are removed and the information is displayed within in an accordion.

About Customer Onboarding

CCustomer Onbaording is an application in the GoCorsicana Portal that allows sales reps, accounts receivable and logistics to on-board new customers with ease.

Reasoning

The process for onboarding new customers has become outdated and inefficient. To mainstream the process and reduce the number of man hours needed to onboard new customers Corsicana Mattress decided to move customer onboarding online.

Objective

To create a mobile responsive application that allows sales reps, accounts receivable, and logistics to on-board new customers.

Approach

To create the customer onboarding application, my senior business analyst and I discussed the current onboarding process with the different departments involved. Once the workflow and requirements were documented we were able to refine both areas to process to make it more efficient. After the final workflow and requirements were decided I was created a few low-fidelity mock-ups of the user interface. My manager and I then agreed on the most efficient user interface to build upon. Once the final high-fidelity mock-up was created, we handed it along with the workflow diagrams to our outsourced development team who handled the development and QA of the application.

Role

  • User Interface Designer
  • Content Designer
  • Project Over Sight

DESIGN

Workflow

Designing an interface means understanding the workflow of an application. The customer onboarding application involved three different departments who each played an intricate part of the process. The final workflow came to existent after multiple iterations of the initial process.

PRODUCT

Design Vs. Reality

High fidelity mock-ups become a template more than a blue print to build from. What is constructed in PhotoShop and XD may look and function better than what is built in HTML5 and CSS3. The final product may be very similar to the final mock-up but there are most likely a few adjustments in the final product.
